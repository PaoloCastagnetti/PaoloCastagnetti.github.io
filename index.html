Murders in Venetia

Funny Tales
"Murders in Venetia is a narrative-driven, investigative VR game that blends immersive storytelling with engaging puzzle-solving. Set in 12th-century Venice, the game transports players into a richly detailed historical world populated by fictional characters and real-world references. As the first interactive film designed for VR devices, it offers a unique experience where players become active participants in the unfolding mystery."

Working on this project from the ground up as a Narrative Designer gave me the opportunity to explore the full depth of interactive storytelling in a VR context. Adapting the narrative from an existing novel challenged me to reconstruct and reinterpret key story elements to better fit the medium, while maintaining coherence and emotional impact. I developed strong skills in scriptwriting, narrative pacing, and historical worldbuilding, and I learned how to create content that remains compelling in an immersive format. Collaborating closely with developers and artists taught me how to align narrative vision with gameplay mechanics and visual style.

Genre: Narrative, investigative, Puzzle
Platform: Meta Quest
Engine: Unity
Team size: ~10
Release date: 05/2025
Currently working since: 04/2024

About Me

Hello, I'm Paolo

My passion for game design was born while playing, driven by a deep curiosity to understand how games are made and a desire to recreate the same emotions I once felt as a player. I chose to pursue this path to combine one of my greatest passions with my need to create something meaningful for others.

For me, game design is all about conveying emotions. I strongly believe that a compelling story is at the heart of any great game, which is why I always put narrative first. My approach is centered on immersion and emotional engagement, crafting experiences that players can not only enjoy but also shape through their choices.

I have experience in system design and I specialise in narrative design, with a particular interest in building rich worlds and memorable characters. I completed a one-year master's in game development followed by a specialization course in narrative design, which helped me grow both technically and creatively.

One of my proudest achievements so far has been writing the full systems, narrative, characters, dialogues, and scripts, for a complete game project, Murders in Venetia.

Looking ahead, I’m excited to contribute to story-driven projects where I can bring to life the kinds of narratives I’ve always dreamed of creating.
